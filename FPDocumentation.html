Voice Activated Photo Taker

Micah Higgins

This project was inspired by the cold winter air that leads me to wearing gloves. But these gloves are not compatible with my phone so I can't take pictures of any beautiful scenery I may come across. A fix for this is voice activated pictures where I don't need to press any buttons with my thick gloves.

Iteration 1

I started by measuring the distances between the iPhones' photo button, it's rotate button and where my two servo motors would go. I then designed a case to hold my phone and the two servos. I also created little pieces to attach to these servos which held screw in fiber mesh tips.
In the video below, I trained my microphone device and designed my code so that if I said the word "Photo" it would cause one servo motor to spin and if I said the word "Rotate" it would cause the other servo motor to spin

After implementing my phone into my projects case, the voice activation and motors worked but the fiber mesh tips making contact with the iPhones buttons did not register as a change in conductivity and my project couldn't take photos.

Iteration 2

For the next and final iteration, I used a stronger motor that pressed the volume buttons on the side of the phone to take a picture, meaning no capacitance would be required. The power comes from a portable charger that connects to my ardunio uno.
